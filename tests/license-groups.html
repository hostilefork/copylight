COPYLIGHT: License groups sample

Sometimes text belongs to a license group. A license group is helpful if your content is actually spread so that it doesn't fall under a single descendant. Let's imagine we don't want a single selection of one of the following divs to trigger a license notification, but selecting several of them should. It becomes clear that the trigger for notification should be a property of the license group...

So here let's say we are taking a few bits from the same Wikipedia article:

Defective by Design is an anti-digital rights management (DRM) initiative by the Free Software Foundation. DRM technology, dubbed "digital restrictions management" by opponents, restricts usersâ€™ ability to freely use their purchased movies, music, literature, software, and hardware in ways they are accustomed to with ordinary non-restricted media (such as books and audio Compact Discs).

And then we copy some more:

The philosophy of the initiative is that DRM is designed to be deliberately defective, to restrict the use of the product. This, they claim, cripples the future of digital freedom. The group aims to target "Big Media, unhelpful manufacturers, and DRM distributors" and to bring public awareness of the issue and increase participation in the initiative.

Then we copy a little more, and think in our heads "wow, people would have to really have their head screwed on backwards to want to live in a world where we can't copy and paste information.

It represents one of the first efforts of the Free Software Foundation to find common cause with mainstream social activists, and to encourage free software advocates to become socially involved. As of late 2006, the campaign was claiming over 12,000 registered members.

What's fun here is to notice how spanning selections in the license group can notice that a significant material selection is being made from a single source, despite that source being split up.
